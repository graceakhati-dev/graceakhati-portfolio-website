🌱 Week 1: From Zero to Possibility

My First Steps in Software Engineering with Power Learn Project

If someone told me a few months ago that I'd be learning software engineering, I probably would have smiled politely and shrugged it off.

Me? In tech? It felt like a distant world reserved for the gifted — not someone like me, with no tech background and a heart rooted in creativity, crochet, and care.

But here I am. One week in. Learning. Growing. Thriving.

And it’s all thanks to the Power Learn Project, a community that has not only opened the door to the world of software development, but also walked with me through it with kindness, structure, and belief.

💻 What I’ve Learned This Week

1. Introduction to Software Engineering

We started by unpacking what software engineering truly is — not just writing code, but solving real-world problems through design, logic, structure, and teamwork. I learned that software engineering is a process, not just a product. That really opened my mind.

2. The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

This was an eye-opener! Understanding the steps involved in planning, designing, developing, testing, and deploying software helped me realize how organized and thoughtful tech creation is. I even began using the SDLC model to manage my first hackathon project — a personal portfolio website. It’s amazing how this model applies to everyday problem-solving too.

3. Basic Tools and Methodologies

We explored the tools developers use, such as Git, VS Code, and GitHub. I now understand version control (kind of like a crochet pattern for your code!), and the importance of documenting your work and collaborating on projects.

4. The Importance of Mindset

Perhaps the most powerful lesson wasn’t technical — it was emotional. I learned that the most important thing I need to succeed in tech is a growth mindset. Mistakes are allowed. Struggling is part of learning. Asking questions isn’t weakness — it’s wisdom.

🌍 My Experience with Power Learn Project

The Power Learn Project is more than a course — it's a community, a support system, a launchpad for African youth to rise in tech. From day one, I felt welcomed, seen, and included. There is a spirit of Ubuntu — "I am because we are" — in every class session, group chat, and mentor message.

Here, you're not just a number on a screen. You're a dreamer being equipped to build solutions for your community and beyond.

I especially loved the way the mentors broke down complex topics into bite-sized lessons, like you're being taught as a beginner — with patience, humor, and heart. As someone who thrives in creative and emotional spaces, this gave me the courage to believe that I do belong in tech.

🧠 Reflections

Before joining PLP, I often felt intimidated by code — it looked like a foreign language. But now, I see it differently. I see it as a new tool for storytelling, expression, and change.

Yes, it’s hard. Yes, it’s unfamiliar. But it’s also deeply rewarding. Every time I understand a new concept, it’s like learning a new stitch in crochet — difficult at first, but beautiful once you see the pattern forming.

📣 To Anyone Thinking of Starting…

If you’ve ever doubted yourself, if you’ve ever felt too old, too late, or too far from tech to start — I’m here to say: it’s not too late.

You are exactly where you need to be to begin.

Start small. Stay curious. Let your passion lead. And when you stumble, remember — you’re not alone. There’s a whole community ready to support you, just like I found in Power Learn Project.
